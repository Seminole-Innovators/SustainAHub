Student Move-Out Guide

Aluminum Cans

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 4

Aluminum Cans are avery recyclable and are accepted by most recycling programs. It is important to make sure that they are cleaned and emptied.

Cardboard Boxes

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 4

Cardboard Boxes are among the most recyclable items. Make sure that when moving out, you place cardboard boxes in the recycling, but always be mindful of recycling labels on boxes.

Glass and Glassware

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 4

Glass and glassware are highly recyclable. Make sure that they any glass items are cleaned out before putting them in the proper recycling, as unclean and unempty glass complicates recycling efforts.

Paper Products

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 4

Paper Products, such as office paper, paper packaging, paper cups WITHOUT waxy interiors, among others, are highly recyclable. Paper cartons, such as milk cartons are NOT easily recyclable. Make sure to be mindful of recycling labels.

Plastic Bags

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 2

Plastic Bags, such as those of grocery stores, need to be taken to special recycle points. They are NOT recyclable through traditional means. Find recycle points here.

Plastic Containers

Recyclability (On scale of 1-4): 3

Plastic Containers are mostly recyclable. This being said, you must make sure that plastic containers are cleaned and emptied out. Unclean plastic containers complicate recycling efforts. Always be mindful of recycling labels on plastics. PET (#1) and HDPE (#2) are highly recyclable and can be trown into traditional recycling.

Here are a list of common plastics and their recyclability:

  • PET (marked #1) polyethylene Terephthalate is used for beverage containers, cleaning containers, and food containers. It is highly recyclable.
  • HDPE (marked #2): High-density polyethylene is a common plastic used for milk cartons, cleaning product bottles, food containers. and other things. It is highly recyclable.
  • PVC (marked #3): Polyvinyl chloride is an inexpensive material used for car parts, containers, and some structural components. It is one of the least recyclable materials and is rarely accepted by standard local recycling.
  • LDPE (marked #4): Low-density polyethylene is a material that is used for most plastic wraps, bags, and food packaging. It is not easily recycled.
  • PP (marked #5): Polypropylene is a durable plastic with a high melting point that's often used for containers that hold hot liquids. Recycling it can be very difficult.
  • PS (marked #6): Polystyrene is used for some packaging and disposable cutlery as well as styrofoam. It is not easily recyclable.
  • Mixed (marked #7): This is a category that refers to mixed or non standard plastics. It is almost impossibe to recycle.
